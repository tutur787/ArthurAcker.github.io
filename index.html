About Me

As a third-year Bachelor of Science student at McGill University, specializing in Statistics and Computer Science, I am deeply passionate about the intersection of data and the vast potential of technology to solve complex problems.
With enthusiasm for mathematics, I thrive on challenges that allow me to dive deep into numbers and algorithms, exploring the hidden patterns that lie within data. My academic journey is complemented by my proficiency in multiple programming languages, including R, where I have utilized the tidyverse package for data analysis, and Python, which has been instrumental in my data science projects.
Beyond the classroom, my interests are wide-ranging. I am an avid soccer player and fan, finding in the sport both the thrill of competition and a fascinating field for data science exploration. My love of soccer extends into a keen interest in soccer data science, where I am eager to apply my analytical skills to uncover insights that can enhance understanding of the game and seek out talents.
Astrophysics is another passion of mine, captivating me with its exploration of the cosmos and the fundamental laws that govern the universe. This interest inspires me to constantly expand my knowledge and seek connections between the vast expanse of space and the data-driven methodologies I am familiar with.
